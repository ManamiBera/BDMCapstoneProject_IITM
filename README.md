# Integrated Operations Enhancement: Processing Delay & Inventory Optimization
Data-driven operational analysis of a manufacturing unit to identify processing delays and improve inventory management.

## Project Overview
This project analyzes operational challenges faced by a manufacturing unit (Balaji Powder Coating), focusing on processing delays and inefficient inventory management.
The analysis was conducted using real operational data collected between April 2025 and September 2025. The goal was to identify performance bottlenecks, understand seasonal impacts on production, and recommend data-driven improvements to operational workflow.

## Business Problem
The company experienced:

•Frequent processing delays

•Poor inventory structure

•Revenue drops during monsoon months

•Inefficient allocation of storage space

These issues impacted order completion time, customer satisfaction, and overall operational efficiency.

## Data Analysis Process
The project included:

•Data Cleaning

•Standardized date formats

•Removed duplicate entries

•Handled missing delivery dates

•Normalized product names

## Analytical Methods Used

•Descriptive statistics

•Trend analysis

•Pareto analysis

•Moving average forecasting

•Volume vs Revenue analysis

## Key Findings
Major insights from the analysis:

•Monsoon months caused a significant drop in revenue and operational efficiency.

•A small number of products contributed to the majority of delays.

•Inventory space was allocated inefficiently.

•High-volume products were not necessarily the most profitable.

## Impact of the Project
The analysis helped propose operational improvements such as:

•Inventory restructuring

•Weather-resilient operational planning

•Pre-monsoon preparation strategy

•Implementation of a centralized data tracking system

These improvements can reduce delays, improve workflow efficiency, and increase operational stability.
